Midtown Ranks #1 in Restaurant Diversity

Manhattan dominates top spots.

Manhattan dominates the top spots for restaurant diversity by neighborhood. The top five neighborhoods are all in the borough with Midtown/Midtown South taking the top spot followed by: the West Village, Hudson Yards/Chelsea/Flat Iron/Union Square, the East Village and Clinton.

Rounding out the bottom of the list were Grymes Hill/Clifton/Fox Hills and Arden Heights in Staten Island, Stuyvesant Town/Cooper Village in Manhattan and Williamsburg in Brooklyn.

The results were calculated using restaurant inspections found on NYC OpenData. The neighborhoods used are based on New York City’s Neighborhood Tabulation Areas. Each neighborhood’s score was based on the amount of different cuisines available.

Source: NYC OpenData
